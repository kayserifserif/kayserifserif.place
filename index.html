REM (2016)

Inspired by personal experiences, this experimental film explores the stages of youth, adolescence, and maturity. Through the use of symbolic imagery and constructed sound, it examines how identity is shaped and transformed throughout life.

The Passage of Days (2016)

The Soviet montage theory, pioneered by Sergei Eisenstein in the 1920s, is an approach to cinema relying heavily on editing. This montage film, based loosely on the Greek poem “The Labyrinth”, depicts an old woman mentally and physically trapped in the past, unable to accept societal change. In particular, it uses metric and tonal editing to highlight the contrast between the character's loneliness in isolation and her liveliness in her relationship with the young child.

Perfect A (2015)

This documentary, featuring interviews and performances from students, parents, and teachers, revolves around the issue of exam-oriented music learning in Hong Kong. It explores the function of music exams and how it affects students' attitudes towards and enjoyment of music, ultimately drawing the conclusion that though music exams can be highly beneficial to music students, a balance must be struck for the student to gain the most out of the experience.
